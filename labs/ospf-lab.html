From Day 1 to OSPF: My Packet Tracer Custom Lab Story

This lab became my running notebook while following Jeremy IT Lab. Instead of building isolated mini-labs, I kept one network alive and upgraded it topic by topic. Every new lesson had to fit into the same topology, so each concept was tested in context: what breaks, what converges, and what I truly understand.

Topology Visualization

The diagram below mirrors the custom Packet Tracer setup: a routed core (R1-R4), multilayer switching on SW3, and access switching on SW1/SW2 with end hosts across multiple VLANs.

How the Lab Evolved (Topic by Topic)

Phase 1: Foundations

I started by naming devices, assigning host IPs/subnet masks/default gateways, and making sure every endpoint had a clean baseline. This taught me to treat consistency as part of troubleshooting.

Phase 2: VLAN Segmentation

I added VLAN 10/20/30, VTP between SW1 and SW2, and trunk links among switches. The big lesson: Layer 2 reachability only works when VLAN membership and trunks agree end-to-end.

Phase 3: Spanning Tree & Security

RSTP root placement, PortFast, and BPDU Guard made the topology stable and predictable. I could finally explain why a link blocks and when it should not.

Phase 4: Inter-VLAN + Routing

I configured SVIs on SW3, routed point-to-point links to routers, and validated host-to-host and router-to-VLAN communication. This phase connected switching concepts with Layer 3 forwarding logic.

Phase 5: OSPF + EtherChannel Integration

Final step was enabling OSPF on all routers and SW3, checking neighbors/adjacency, then adding EtherChannel from SW1/SW2 to SW3. This is where everything clicked: resilient Layer 2 paths feeding a dynamic Layer 3 control plane.

Milestone Checklist

  1. Configured hostnames on all devices.
  2. Configured PC default gateways, IP addresses, and subnet masks.
  3. Configured VLAN 10/20/30 and VTP on SW1-SW2.
  4. Configured trunk ports on SW1, SW2, and SW3.
  5. Configured routed point-to-point links from SW3 toward routed domain.
  6. Configured RSTP on SW1/SW2/SW3 with root bridge planning.
  7. Added PortFast and BPDU Guard on access-facing interfaces.
  8. Configured SVIs on SW3 and validated inter-VLAN routing.
  9. Validated cross-VLAN endpoint connectivity with ping tests.
  10. Configured router interfaces and addressing plan.
  11. Enabled OSPF on all routers and SW3; confirmed adjacency.
  12. Validated routed reachability to all VLANs and hosts.
  13. Configured EtherChannel uplinks from SW1/SW2 toward SW3.

Reflection

My biggest takeaway is that labbing is where memorization becomes understanding. OSPF neighbors forming is easy to watch, but knowing why they form quickly in a stable switched topology took layering every previous topic correctly.

By building on one topology from Day 1 up to OSPF, I can now troubleshoot with a sequence in mind: check VLAN/trunk/STP first, then SVI and routed interfaces, then dynamic routing state. That mindset is what made this custom lab valuable.
